Women Empowerment

Women are vulnerable when it comes to well being of mental and physical health as a result of the huge burden of work. Women empowerment is achieved when the economic, political, social identity of women is recognised and incorporated into existing systems.
-RLHP has been giving a platform for women to take initiative and solve community level issues through lobbying with the government, monitoring of schools, interventions with social organizations etc. Training and capacity building for women livelihood upliftment are also conducted. -Women federations like DWANI, BELAKU and other groups work together for protecting women from marital abuse and empower their life with dignity, respect and equality

Livelihood opportunities

Livelihood enhancement for women with the objective of making them self reliant and economically independent is the ultimate goal of RLHP. To achieve this goal, RLHP conducts training, promotes small businesses for women entrepreneurship and helps with income generation activities like running a petty shop, saree shop and practising floriculture and animal husbandry. SHGs, widow and destitute women who have benefitted from various IGP programmes are now earning better amounts and have improved the social and economic status of their families.

Domestic Worker’s Forum

Domestic Worker’s Forum

To make women domestic workers aware about their legal rights, RLHP forms groups in slums and conducts capacity building sessions. The training sessions revolve around the topics of labour laws, social security schemes, leadership, personality development, entrepreneurship and life skills. -RLHP has been successful in providing assistance and acquiring smart cards from the labour department and pensions to women domestic workers. -RHLP also conducts employer’s meetings, where owners and union leaders of companies and domestic workers participate. Sensitization regarding employer’s responsibility, labour laws, domestic worker’s rights etc are done.
