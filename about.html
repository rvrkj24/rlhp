About Us

Founded by a group of dedicated and like minded people, the Non-Governmental Organisation - Rural Literacy and Health Programme (RLHP), operates in an integrated manner with a major focus on the development of underprivileged and deprived sections of the society. The aim of RLHP is to contribute to the holistic development of people living in slums and villages.

History Vision Mission Approach
History

History

RLHP was founded by Mrs. Nirmala Jayraj, Mr. Joy Maliekal and Mrs. Philomena Joy. Situated in the southern part of India , RLHP was registered in 1984 under the Karnataka Societies Registration Act 1960. With an aim of comprehensive development of people living in slums and villages, RLHP adopted an integrated approach with creative and innovative solutions to benefit the larger community. The developmental interventions were designed with a human centric approach and ensures people’s participation in our interventions on child and youth development, women empowerment and community-based health & sanitation.

Vision

Vision

“A sustainable society where people are treated with dignity, equity, and justice with a right to live freely”

Mission

Mission

To empower the marginalized poor in slums and villages, vulnerable children and others affected by natural calamities and climate changes, and to respond to the challenges of growing poverty and human rights violations through collective and affirmative action leading to sustainable development, self-reliance and economic independence.

Approach

Approach

We ideate and initiate novel interventions by championing development of the people in slums and villages with a child focused and right based approach. Our extension and consolidation strength allows us to partner with similar groups and transform the lives of millions on the ground.
