Child Development

RLHP adopts a holistic approach for the development of children in slums and villages. The core activities are centered around the provision of ensuring quality education for children, formation of Child Rights Clubs, running library programmes, rehabilitating children from dire circumstances and ensuring greater outreach through mass awareness activities. The welfare of vulnerable and needy children are catered to in a need-based manner. RLHP is committed to

child and rights-based impact.

Rehabilitation Homes

Rehabilitation Homes

RLHP has estaiblished two shelter homes in 1999 in Mysore- Innam uttantahalli - Ashakirana (Ray of Hope) for boys and Ashabhavana-Srirampura (House of Hope) for girls - to provide safe environment to children who are orphaned, malnourished and estranged due to poverty, domestic violence, trafficking, sexual abuse, etc.
-Integrated rehabilitation care is given to the children by providing food, shelter, clothing, healthcare, value education, counselling support and life skills. The children get the opportunity to actively engage in sports, yoga, meditation, computer usage and cultural

events and develop their inner competencies and creativity. They also engage in crucial discussions related to social and environmental issues.
-Children at shelter homes are enrolled into government schools and engaged in various experiential workshops and summer camps. Furthermore, children are supported and encouraged to be self-reliant and pursue higher studies after 12th grade. RLHP supports them by providing vocational training support in specialised courses like Engineering, Law, Nursing and Lab Technician.

Eradication of Child Labour

Eradication of Child Labour

Children are forced to work in harsh conditions and instead of attending school, often end up with detrimental physical and mental health problems such as depression, premature ageing and drug addictions. -RLHP advocates for the basic human rights of those children by organizing awareness camps on child rights and CLRPA, so that the street and migrant children can be prevented from being trapped in the clutches child labour. -RLHP liaises with like-minded organisations, various state and district level stakeholders and supports them in preparing intensive action plans to confront and prohibit child labour within their operational areas.

Childline

Childline 1098

CHILDLINE 1098 responds to emergency needs of children in crisis including child labourers, street children and victims of trafficking, exploitation, substance abusers, child marriage, etc. -With the support of the Childline India Foundation, Ministry of Women and Child Development and other NGOs, the Child Collab Center at RLHP has been running for the past 10 years and ensuring 24x7 immediate safety and rehabilitation services to children in crises. -RLHP organises street plays, cycle rallies, distributes handbills and erects stalls in schools and public places to sensitise people about the significance of the 24/7 toll free helpline service of CHILDLINE 1098.

Quality education for children

Quality education for children

RLHP believes in providing every child the basic right of education. RLHP has undertaken multiple initiatives to support quality learning opportunities for every child with a special focus on children who are bound to be left out due to social and economical circumstances.

Supplementary Education Centers

Supplementary Education Centres (SEC) were initiated in the year 2000 with the prime objective to improve the quality of education, sustain children’s interest in education, and prevent school dropout and child labour. The SECs are now functional in 15 villages and 10 slums and cater to 600 children. 5th to 10th standard students participate in extracurricular activities and get trained in the domain of their interest and potential under the guidance of our trained teachers. Tuition support is provided for 2-3 hours in the evenings to assist students in clearing their doubts and completing their homeworks. Children facing difficulties in school tuition are given reading and writing enhancement programmes. Regular monitoring helps us keep track of the overall learning progress of children and sustain efforts towards quality education. The average % of marks obtained by SEC students in the 10th standard public examinations has improved from below 50% to 86% in the past 9 years.
Children are motivated and guided to choose their career wisely. Students who pass the 10th standard are assisted with career counselling and training programmes. Health and educational programmes are conducted in tent-schools for child labourers and children from migrant families. RLHP restores drinking water facilities in such schools and supports the children with educational materials including books, pens, pencils, bags, uniforms, LCD computers.

Children’s Participation

Children’s Participation

Child Rights Clubs and Federations

Through Vidhyodaya children's clubs and Gnanodhaya children's federation, participation of children is ensured at various levels of governance at home, school and community. Regular meetings are held and training on leadership, POCSO Act, JJ Act, RTE Act, gender, protection of environment, civic sense and life skills are given to children. Children from challenging communities are encouraged to actively participate in Child Rights Clubs, Green Teams, SDMCs, Makkala Gram Sabhas, Panchayat meetings, Visiting Govt. offices, and also Local Governance for the overall development of the children in urban & rural areas. Children club members continue to publish the Makkala Dwani Newsletter and bringing to the notice of the public, the issues on Child Rights Violations, Lack of Quality Education, Corporal Punishment, Implementation of RTE, Community and School issues, through writing articles for the newsletter, and making aware the community, Govt. officials, panchayat, school and SDMC on the issues the schools and school children are facing.
