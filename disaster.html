COVID 19

COVID 19

The whole world is reeling under the impact of COVID 19. Shutdown of trades has forced millions to lose their jobs and fall into a financial and survival trap. India witnessed workers from unorganized sectors, migrant labourers and families running out of food and bare necessities. -Home quarantine needs of the marginalized communities of Mysore district were identified and food items and sanitization essentials were distributed. -RLHP provided psychosocial support and ran Individual Education Programmes (IEP) for 2000 children to ensure joyful learning even during school lockdown. -4000 families from Mysore slums and villages were equipped with grocery kits and health care materials. -400 farmers were supported with saplings and seeds to resume their agriculture practice

Kerala Flood

Kerala Flood

In 2018, torrential rain ravaged the state of Kerala, causing floods and landslides in all 14 districts. Concerned about the struggles of the inhabitants amidst the natural calamity, RLHP ran flood relief operations in Wayanad and Alleppey. The project from November 2018 to June 2019 executed relief and rehabilitation programmes for the victim including extending livelihood opportunities such as cash for work, vocational training programme for youths, restoration of five community rainwater harvesting structures, 20 public water stand posts, distribution of 250 water storage containers, kitchen utensils and chicken for women-headed families, completion of household and school toilet and WASH facilities, and drinking water units for schools and anganwadis. With collaboration of teachers and community members, flood-affected children were supported with play materials, psycho-social counselling, drawing campaigns etc.

Tsunami Rehab

Tsunami Rehab

RLHP initiated a recovery and rehabilitation programme in 2004 for the Tsunami affected communities of Poombukar, Neidhavasal and Thenpathi of Nagapattinam District in Tamil Nadu. The prime focus was on immediately restoring livelihood activities of the inhabitants. Priority was also given to health & sanitation and psychological well-being through regular health camps, health education, door to door counselings, psycho-social support to children and women, capacity building for youth, etc.
