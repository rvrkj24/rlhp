Child Development

Rehabilitation home for children

Home Away From Home

Rajeshwari. M, joined Ashabhavana at the age of 6. Her family, including her mother and her 4 year old sister, Priyanka, used to beg and live on the streets. The children were malnourished and in critical health condition. RHLP’s outreach programmes identified and counselled these children and offered them accommodation at Ashabhavana, a home away from home. Initially, their mother used to visit them at Ashabhavana but stopped coming eventually.The children were provided with supplementary food and extra educational support and they quickly adapted and started living a better life with a focus to be self-reliant. After completing schooling, Rajeshwari joined as a home nurse in June 2016 at Ganesha Clinic in Mysore while her sister continued pursuing an education.

Rajeshwari also follows her interests in classical dancing like Bharatnatyam, Kamsale and art work. RLHP has changed the lives of hundreds of children with rehabilitation support and will continue to do the same till this challenge comes to an end.

Childline

A Call For Hope

In 2011, Narayan, a 14 year old orphan boy who worked as a child labourer at a house, was found wandering the streets of Mysore. On witnessing that the boy was physically and mentally tortured by the family, the neighbours called the 1098 RLHP Childline and reported the plight of the boy. RHLP, with the support of a government-allied system, rescued the boy and brought him under the care of Child Welfare Committee. He was later given shelter at Ashakirana Shelter Home on request of the committee. Initially, he had difficulty adjusting to the new environment and was weak and unhealthy.

He could not socialize with the other children, but with continuous love and counselling support, there was a change in his behaviour and he was able to start mingling with the other children. After five months of counselling and care, he was enrolled in the 7th standard where he continued his studies for SSLC exam and went on to score first class. Narayan had an interest in cooking and dreamt of becoming a chef. He was thus given an opportunity to join a Hotel Management course at St. Philomena College. He successfully completed his education and secured a good paid job at Green Hotel, Mysuru, with a starting salary of Rs.6000 per month. Narayan continues to be a part of shelter home activities and believes in giving back to the welfare of children and society.

Childline

A Story Of Never Looking Back

Bhairavi, a 9 year old child from Ashokapuram, Mysore, was in need of immediate care. Her father had suffered a paralysis stroke. Bhairavi’s mother had abandoned her when she was 4 year old and she was totally dependent on her grandmother. On 5th February 2020, RLHP received a message at around 6.25 PM about an orphaned girl who needs shelter and rescue. With the help of concerned officials and police, the girl was rescued and admitted to KR Hospital Sakhi One Staff Centre. During counselling, the girl opened up and reported about being sexually assaulted by four strangers. Police took immediate action by arresting the culprits and taking them to court.

Bhairavi, with a fresh start, is now staying in government shelter homes with a dream of having a good life ahead. RLHP is helping her with all means to support her education and welfare.

Supplementary Education Centres

Sulthana's story of not giving up

“RLHP’s supplementary education and career guidance session were very useful for me and helped me a lot.” says Sulthana, 16 year old teenager residing in N.H.Palya slum. Sulthana’s family of seven lived in a small rented house. She studied at government school and had difficulty understanding English and Mathematics. Hailing from an economically vulnerable family, she couldn't afford extra tuition. RLHP initiated a Supplementary Education Centre in N.H.Palya Slum, and Sulthana , along with 15 other students, received guidance for 10th std exam from an experienced teacher. Career guidance and motivation from RLHP staff to the SSLC students gave them a direction for a successful career.

Development Through Education Enhancement

A Road Towards Education

Muskan, a 10 year old girl from Gowsiyanagar Slum, lived with her step-mother in Mysore as her father worked in a scrap factory in Bangalore. She was in 5th std when she started suffering from a skin disease. Despite her critical health, Muskan was forced into doing household chores by her step-mother. Muskan’s health was neglected and her skin condition worsened, which also affected her education. The severity of skin condition ultimately forced her to quit school. As soon as RLHP learned about her plight, immediate help was provided.

Muskan was taken to a hospital and given medical care. Apart from the treatment and care, RLHP Staff motivated and counselled the step-mother to help Muskan and support her education. After completely recovering from the disease, Muskan started attending school regularly with full support from her family. RLHP not only provides medical support but also invests time in providing moral support and counselling wherever needed and continues to serve its part for the well-being of children.

Child Rights Clubs

Rusksar’s Story Of Dedication And Commitment For Education

Ruksar Banu, an active member of CRC from Sathgalli slum had to discontinue her studies due to her vulnerable economic background. Her father was an auto driver and her mother was working as a domestic help and couldn't really contribute much for Ruksar. Being an intelligent student and having passed her SSLC examination with first class, Ruskar underwent the difficult task of leaving studies. She discussed this with the RLHP members during a CRC meeting and an initiative was taken to support her education.Ruskar's parents were counselled and sensitised about the importance of girl child education by the team. She received support from her family to get admission into a government college and even purchased new uniforms and books.

This created hope for other girls who dreamt of having a career and being successful. With immense gratitude towards CRC, CRMC and SHG of Sathgalli, Rusksar started her education journey to fulfill her dreams of becoming a teacher. RLHP continues to be a ray of hope for education to girls like Ruskar and many more.

From A Difficult Childhood To Helping Mankind

Madhu Kumar, stayed at his grandmother’s house post his parents demise. Living with his grandmother and his uncle and aunt was not easy. He was beaten for no reason and forced to run household errands. He was admitted to Ashakirana to keep him safe. Having joined as a physically weak child, Madhu eventually became healthy and started socializing with the other boys. RLHP staff admitted him to a nearby school in 3rd std. Although he had difficulty in reading and writing, he showed interest in other programmes like life skills, personality development, health and hygiene, organic agriculture etc.

Regular guidance by RLHP staff brought changes in him. He cleared the SSLC examination in 2016, joined PUC and pursued a three year general nursing course at Chennarayapattana. With gratitude towards RLHP and the dream to serve mankind, Madhu continues to work hard to achieve his goals.

Women empowerment

Domestic Workers Forum

From A Victim Of Domestic Violence To Becoming Financially Independent

Lakshmi, a resident of Manjunathpura slum, Mysore, lived with her three children and her husband, a construction worker. Being a habitual drinker, he would quarrel with Lakshmi and get physically violent towards her. After reporting this to the RLHP team, Surya Kanti Domestic Workers Group and DWANI Women Federation leaders, her in-laws and husband were counselled regarding the economic situation of the family and children’s education.

The team informed her husband about the Domestic Violence Bill. Lakshmi later joined a government school as an assistant cook and started earning well. Being the voice for a cause like women empowerment is RLHP’s forte and was when they helped Lakshmi to be independent and live freely. This was an inspiration for other women who were victims of domestic violence and got the courage to report against it.

Livelihood Oppurtunity For Rural Women

Bhagyamma’s Journey Towards Entrepreneurship

Bhagyamma, resides with her four daughters and her husband at Srirangapatna Taluk, Mandya District. Her husband sold coconut on bicycle. Due to financial constraints, one of her daughters was married off and others had to drop out from school. With a loan of Rs.10,000 provided by RLHP under IGP, she purchased a push cart for selling tender coconuts near her home. Apart from this, she also got into coconut business with her husband. The money earned by Bhagyamma’s was utilised for repaying loans and funding the education of her daughters. RLHP has helped many women to get loans and become entrepreneurs, and continues the work of making women financially independent.

People Development

Power Of Awareness Which Paved A Way For Livelihood Opportunities

Livelihood opportunities are vulnerable for the inhabitants of Kuthnuru village because of which they migrate to Kerala every year for better employment opportunities. The villagers were suffering from lack of transportation, safe drinking water, sanitation and health care facilities and lived with minimal amenities. Gram Panchayat members and authorities were not taking any measure to improve the well-being of the villagers even when there was an increased prevalence of child labour, child marriage and domestic violence. With the intervention of RLHP in 2018, the villagers became aware of government schemes that they weren't aware of before and started supporting available programmes.

RLHP’s frequent input sessions, training, awareness generation programmes, public meetings etc. made a remarkable change in the social living of the inhabitants. On learning about MNREGA, people started applying for job cards and started working under the scheme from the beginning of 2019. The villagers also started identifying their needs and drew the attention of government authorities towards their concerns. RLHP ensures good governance, awareness generation and keeps motivating the people to gain livelihood opportunities by such interventions.

From Being Unemployed To Becoming A Facilitator For Others.

Shivakumar, from Gore Bal Village, is one of the members of BRSSG and attends RLHP training programmes regularly. His aged parents had applied for funds under the government scheme “Ashraya” but had not received the fund even after 5 years. Shivakumar visited many government offices regularly with no adequate response. He raised this issue in one of the skill training sessions. The RLHP team informed him about RTI and how to file an RTI. As soon as RTI was filed, government officials started responding and his family finally received the fund of Rs. 75,000/- .

This experience created confidence in Shivakumar and he facilitated 60 job cards to community members. Using own job card under MNREGA, he worked on his 0.5 acre land and planted saplings of high-demand, consumable items like curry leaves, lemon and drumstick that eventually yielded good returns and brought him a profitable income, securing his financial status.

Community-based Health and Sanitation

Health Clinics and Health camps

Prajwal’s journey from being malnourished to healthy child

Prajwal, a 3.7 year-old child from Hosalli village was identified as weak during the field visit by the RLHP team members. While his ideal weight should have been 15.5 kg for a child his age, he weighed 3 kg less as per the initial assessment. The family was not aware of the need for nutritious food. The RLHP team oriented the family on nutritious food and its significance in maintaining the physical and mental wellbeing of the child. RLHP also equipped the family with nutrient rich foods items like ragi and eggs and followed up regularly to track Prajwal’s progress. At present, Prajwal eats more nutritious food and is physically more active and has gained weight. His parents are providing more nutritious mixtures and food for the child and are very much happy with the improvement.

Escaped Child Marriage: Living a happy life.

“In the year 1998, RLHP members approached me, and I joined Asha Bhavana where my journey towards a better life began. I lost my mother when I was 2 year old, and hence, stayed at my uncle’s place in Kollegala, where I was treated like a domestic servant. When I recall my childhood days, I see darkness. Kollegala, a small village, had people with blind beliefs and child marriage was common. I have seen young girls get married and become mothers of two-three kids. When I was 10 years old, I used to attend programmes conducted by RLHP regarding awareness about child issues. These programmes made me think about my rights and in the year 1998, my aunt sent me with the RLHP team and I started my education journey. I ended up doing a D.Ed course and a diploma in tailoring which landed me a job in a government hostel. I also worked with RLHP for a while. I’m a mother to two daughters now. I married Jagadeesh, a paint contractor. I met him through my mother-in-law, who is a member of Dwani Federation. With opportunities given by RLHP, I now have a free and happy life, and as RLHP continues its good cause, I would be contributing in any way possible.”

Lalita, Mysore
